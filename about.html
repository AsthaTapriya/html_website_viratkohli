Virat Kohli: From a Fiery Youngster to a Cricketing Legend

It is said, 'Greatness isn’t given, it’s earned with passion, discipline, and perseverance.' Virat Kohli’s journey is a story of relentless dedication, fearless leadership, and an unshakable desire to win. He didn’t just play the game—he forever changed the mindset of the Indian cricket team, instilling a fearless and winning attitude.

The above lines must have given you a hint about Virat Kohli’s personality. This personality hasn't made in a day.Through sheer dedication and hard work, he shaped himself into who he is today.To know about his hard work let's take a deep dive into his journey and start it from beginning :

EARLY LIFE

✔ Virat Kohli was born on 5 November 1988 in Delhi into a Punjabi Hindu family.His mother Saroj Kohli is as a housewife while his father Prem Nath Kohli worked as a criminal lawyer.He has an elder brother Vikas and an elder sister Bhawna.
✔In 1998, the West Delhi Cricket Academy was created. His father arranged for him to meet Rajkumar Sharma. Upon the suggestions, Kohli's father considered enrolling his son in a professional cricket academy, as they believed his ability merited more than gully cricket.He was unable to secure a place in the U-14 Delhi team, due to extraneous factors. His father reportedly received offers for influential clubs, which would ensure his selection, but he declines.
✔His life took a dramatic turn on December 18, 2006, when he lost his father.Kohli was an 18-year-old playing for Delhi in the Ranji Trophy. He was in the middle of a crucial match against Karnataka when his father passed away. Despite his father’s death, he did not leave his team. Next morning, he went and batted for Delhi. His unbeaten 90 runs saved his team from defeat.This incident became a defining moment in his life and career, shaping him into the relentless cricketer he is today.

INTERNATIONAL CAREER

✔Virat Kohli made his international debut in 2008 after leading India to victory in the 2008 U-19 World Cup. Initially playing as a middle-order batsman,he cemented his place in the team with consistent performances. His breakthrough came during the 2011 World Cup, where he scored a century in his debutmatch and played a crucial role in India’s triumph. In 2013, he became the fastest Indian to score an ODI century, showcasing his aggressive yet controlled batting style. His adaptability across formats made him India’s batting mainstay.
✔From 2014 to 2019, Kohli dominated world cricket, setting numerous records. He became India’s Test captain in 2014 and later took over as the limited-overs captain in 2017. Under his leadership, India achieved historic milestones, including a Test series win in Australia (2018-19). Kohli’s ability to chase under pressure earned him the nickname “Chase Master”, His 71 international centuries by 2022 placed him among cricket’s greatest.
✔Kohli stepped down as T20I captain in 2021 and later as Test and ODI captain in 2022, but he remains a key player for India. He regained form in 2022-23, scoring his 71st century after a long drought and continued to be a dominant force in global cricket.
✔In the 2023 ICC Cricket World Cup, Virat Kohli delivered an extraordinary performance, amassing 765 runs—the highest in a single edition of the tournament. He achieved three centuries and maintained an impressive average of 95.62. Notably, Kohli scored his 50th ODI century during the semi-final against New Zealand, surpassing Sachin Tendulkar's record of 49 ODI centuries. His consistent excellence earned him the Player of the Tournament award.
✔In 2024, Kohli played a pivotal role in India's T20 World Cup victory. His match-winning performance in the final earned him the Man of the Match award. Following this triumph, Kohli announced his retirement from T20 Internationals, concluding his T20I career on a high note.
✔The 2025 ICC Champions Trophy faced challenges where India's matches were played in Dubai.Despite these hurdles, Kohli showcased his leadership and batting prowess, guiding India to victory in the semi-final against Australia with a crucial 84-run innings. India went on to win the tournament, securing their first 50-over ICC trophy since 2013.

IPL FRANCHISE CAREER

✔Kohli was picked by Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) in 2008 as a young U-19 star. While he wasn’t an immediate standout, he gradually became a key player. By 2011, he was the only player retained by RCB and finished as the team's highest run-scorer that season.
✔ Kohli became RCB’s full-time captain in 2013. His peak IPL season came in 2016, where he shattered records by scoring 973 runs with four centuries, leading RCB to the final. This remains the highest runs tally in a single IPL season.
✔ Despite Kohli’s consistent performances, RCB failed to win an IPL title. His standout season in 2018 saw him cross 5,000 IPL runs, making him the fastest to do so. In 2021, RCB reached the playoffs, but Kohli stepped down as captain after the season, ending his eight-year leadership tenure.
✔After a dip in form in 2022, Kohli bounced back in IPL 2023, smashing two back-to-back centuries and finishing among the top run-scorers. He became the first player to score 7,000 runs in IPL history, solidifying his legacy as the league's most consistent batter.
✔ Kohli has been with RCB for 17 years, never playing for any other franchise. Despite the team’s lack of an IPL title, his loyalty, leadership, and unmatched batting records have made him the face of RCB. He remains a fan favorite and one of the most iconic players in IPL history.
